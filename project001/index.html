  • Our animal friends at the centre are doing well!

    The animals at our shelter are steadily recovering with warm care. From eagle owls to kestrels and swans, many are improving through expert treatment. Please continue to cheer them on until they’re ready to return to nature.

    Join us this Saturday, May 11, for an adoption info session at the KARMA shelter. You’ll have a chance to meet adoptable animals and ask any questions you have about adoption. No registration required — everyone is welcome!

    With the warm spring sunshine, our animals are enjoying more walks and bath time. Watching them stretch and play in the sun brings a peaceful rhythm to the shelter's day.

  • Help Us Give Wings Again

    A rescued kestrel is now in stable recovery. After losing the ability to fly due to a wing injury, this brave bird is now practicing short flights again. Please cheer it on as it prepares to return to the wild.

  • Year-End Giving with KARMA

    This winter, KARMA is hosting a "Share the Warmth" year-end donation event. All proceeds will go toward food, medical care, and winter supplies for shelter animals. Let’s close out the year by sharing warmth and kindness together.

  • Winter Wildlife Campaign & Volunteer Drive

    Even in the cold season, wildlife in nature needs our care. Through the "Protect the Winter Forest" campaign, KARMA is looking for volunteers to help with feeding, fence checks, and other outdoor tasks until December. Join us in this small but meaningful act for nature.

What to Know When Rescuing

Always prioritise safety — protect both yourself and the animal. If you find a stray or wild animal, contact a local rescue centre or expert right away. Use protective tools like gloves, blankets, or a carrier to handle the animal gently. Check the animal’s injury or condition quickly and document if needed. Move the animal to a safe and secure location to prevent injury or escape. Before giving food or water, assess the animal’s condition and proceed with care.

Korea Animal Rescue & Management Association

KARMA is a non-profit organisation officially approved by the Ministry of Environment.
Located in Yangju, Gyeonggi-do, we operate a 3,000-pyeong animal protection centre and headquarters dedicated to the preservation of life and nature.

Founded in 1998 with support from the Korea Forest Service and Korea Tobacco & Ginseng Corporation,
KARMA now provides comprehensive rescue, treatment, protection, and adoption services for abandoned animals.

We care for around 10,000 animals each year.
Through medical treatment and safe shelter, rescued animals are given a second chance at life.

With 8 rescue vehicles and more than 30 staff members,
we work to prevent poaching and animal abandonment, promote adoptions, and raise awareness about coexistence between animals and humans.

We also support endangered species restoration efforts and collaborate with global animal welfare organisations
to help build a better world for all living beings.

KARMA exists to protect every life’s right to live in happiness.
