About Me

I began my career as a broadcast editor,
where I developed a sharp eye for structuring messages and guiding visual flow.

I excel at quickly identifying problems and translating practical solutions into clear visuals.

Today, I focus on designing deliberate and user-contextual digital experiences.

Experience

2013 - 2018

Worked as a broadcast post-production director,
overseeing the entire technical process to shape visuals and deliver messaging effectively.
Handled all aspects of technical quality—subtitling, colour correction, timing, sound, and final output.
Collaborated with brand design teams to implement and finalise deliverables.

2019 - 2023

Worked as a barista and store manager in Korea and the UK,
where I developed a strong sensitivity to user behaviour by interacting with diverse customers daily.

2024 – Present

Transitioned into a UX/UI designer, focusing on designing digital products rooted in brand experience.
Skilled in information architecture and user flow design,
and equipped with hands-on web publishing and implementation abilities.

Strengths

Experienced in information architecture and user flow design.

Able to design logically across system and UI component levels.

Familiar with the full process from planning to design to implementation.

Focus on delivering outcomes that balance functionality and visual clarity.

Off the Record

My favourite ice cream brand during overseas trips had plenty of charm,

but its value wasn’t clearly communicated in Korea — this became the starting point of the Ben & Jerry’s redesign.

A simple empathy — “I love animals” — evolved into deeper reflection on how we share information,

eventually taking form as the KARMA web & mobile renewal project.

My time at a K-pop club in London, where I met people from various backgrounds,

inspired me to rethink fan culture in a more open and structured way — which led to the Jennie fan page design.
